ANGELA ALIOTO, one of America's most outspoken political activists, turns her laser-sharp focus on the American political process, confirming our worst fears about influence peddling and governmental favors for sale. Drawing on her eight years as a prominent politician and a continuous thorn in the side of the political establis hment, she constructs modern politics as a parallel to Danté's Inferno, and pulls no punches in showing why government and politics aren't working like they should.
